History

Historical Background

- Built in 1892 as a nurses’ quarters for the Government Civil Hospital.
- 1939 onwards: Converted into a mental hospital for women, with rumors that it was once used as a leprosarium.
- Japanese occupation (1941–1945): Taken over by the Japanese military police, allegedly used for interrogation and executions.
- Post-war until 1971: Continued to function as a psychiatric hospital.
- After 1971: The building was abandoned for nearly two decades, fueling ghost stories and urban legends.
- 1998: Redeveloped into the Sai Ying Pun Community Complex, with the granite façade and arched verandahs preserved.
- 2015: Declared a statutory monument.
- 2023: The arched verandahs were opened to the public free of charge.


High Street Haunted House

- Crying and singing voices: Residents claimed to hear eerie sounds at night.
- Ghostly apparitions: Tales of headless figures and shadows drifting along the corridors.
- Basement myths: The underground car park is rumored to have been a morgue, with one particular spot (“C44”) said to bring misfortune.
- Execution site: Stories suggest Japanese forces executed prisoners here, adding to its haunted reputation.
